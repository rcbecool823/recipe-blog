A warm, nourishing bowl of wonton soup

  • ☐ 1 package square wonton wrappers
  • ☐ 8 oz. ground pork (fatty preferred)
  • ☐ 1 ½ inch ginger root
  • ☐ 2 ½ tbsp sesame oil
  • ☐ 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • ☐ salt
  • ☐ black pepper
  • ☐ 1 scallion (chopped)
  • ☐ 1 egg wash

(Optional) 10 oz. baby bok choy or similar greens

Dunk the ginger root into a bowl of water to soften it and season the water for flavoring the wonton mix. After it has sat for over an hour, finely dice the ginger.

In a medium bowl, add the finely chopped ginger along with the bowl of water, ground pork, sesame oil, black pepper, soy sauce, and salt. Mix thoroughly until emulsified, almost like a paste.

Fill a small bowl with water. Grab a wrapper and dip your finger in the water to moisten the edges. (Egg wash works too.)

Place about 1 teaspoon of the pork mixture in the center of each wrapper. Avoid overfilling to ensure proper sealing.

Fold the wrapper in half and press to seal. Bring the back edge toward you and connect the two bottom corners with a dab of water or egg wash.

Cook or freeze the wontons immediately. To freeze, place on a lined sheet and freeze until solid before transferring to freezer bags (up to 3 months). No need to thaw before cooking.

Boil and taste a few wontons to adjust seasoning if needed. Continue assembling until all filling is used, placing wontons on a lined sheet to prevent sticking.

Bring a pot of water to boil, create a whirlpool, and add the wontons one at a time. Once they float, cook for another 1-2 minutes.

Remove with a slotted spoon, place in bowls, pour soup over them, and garnish with scallions, bok choy, soy sauce, sesame oil, and optional chili oil.

Enjoy ♡
